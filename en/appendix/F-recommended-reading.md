# Appendix F: Recommended Reading

> This appendix lists core deep learning reference resources, organized by difficulty level.

---

## F-1 Core Textbooks

### Beginner

| Book | Author | Why Read |
|:-----|:-------|:---------|
| *Dive into Deep Learning* | Zhang, Lipton, Li, Smola | Hands-on, free online |
| *Neural Networks and Deep Learning* | Michael Nielsen | Clear intuition, free online |
| *Deep Learning with Python* | François Chollet | Keras-focused, practical |

### Intermediate

| Book | Author | Why Read |
|:-----|:-------|:---------|
| *Deep Learning* (Goodfellow et al.) | Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville | The "bible" of deep learning |
| *Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning* | Christopher Bishop | Mathematical depth |

### Advanced

| Book | Author | Why Read |
|:-----|:-------|:---------|
| *Probabilistic Machine Learning* | Kevin Murphy | Comprehensive modern treatment |
| *The Elements of Statistical Learning* | Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman | Classic statistical learning |

## F-3 Research Papers

### Must-Read Classics

| Paper | Year | Contribution |
|:------|:-----|:-------------|
| *ImageNet Classification with Deep CNNs* (AlexNet) | 2012 | Deep learning breakthrough |
| *Deep Residual Learning* (ResNet) | 2015 | Skip connections |
| *Attention Is All You Need* (Transformer) | 2017 | Attention mechanism |
| *BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers* | 2018 | Pre-training paradigm |
| *Language Models are Few-Shot Learners* (GPT-3) | 2020 | Scaling law |
